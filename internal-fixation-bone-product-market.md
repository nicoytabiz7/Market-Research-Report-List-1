<p><ul><li>Plate</li><li>Screws</li><li>Nail</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The internal fixation bone product market consists of various types of devices used to stabilize broken bones. Plates are flat metal constructs that are attached to the bone surface to hold fragments together. Screws are cylindrical fasteners that secure bone pieces or plates in place. Nails are elongated rods inserted into the medullary cavity of long bones to provide support during healing. Collectively, these products enhance recovery outcomes by promoting proper bone alignment and stability during the healing process.</p></p>
