<p><p>The semi-fermented tea market is categorized into three types based on the fermentation levels. Light semi-fermented teas, with 10% to 20% fermentation, maintain a fresh and floral profile. Medium semi-fermented teas, ranging from 20% to 50%, offer a balance of freshness and depth, characterized by richer flavors. Heavy semi-fermented teas, with fermentation levels between 50% and 80%, exhibit bold, robust characteristics, often resembling fully fermented teas. Each type appeals to different consumer preferences, influencing market dynamics and demand.</p></p>
