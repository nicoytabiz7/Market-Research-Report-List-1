<p><p>The antifreeze or coolant market is primarily categorized into three types: Ethylene Glycol (EG), Propylene Glycol (PG), and glycerine. EG is widely used for its excellent thermal properties and low cost, making it the most common coolant in automotive applications. PG, being non-toxic, is preferred for food-grade and environmentally sensitive applications. Glycerine, derived from plant oils, finds niche usage in specialized cooling systems where biodegradability is essential. Each type addresses specific requirements based on toxicity, efficiency, and environmental impact.</p></p>
