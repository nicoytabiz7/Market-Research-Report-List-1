<p><p>The optical transceiver market consists of various form factors, each designed for specific applications. SFF (Small Form Factor) and SFP (Small Form-Factor Pluggable) are compact modules suitable for data centers and networking. QSFP (Quad Small Form-Factor Pluggable) supports higher bandwidth, ideal for dense networking. CFP (C Form-Factor Pluggable) is used in high-speed applications, while XFP (10 Gigabit Small Form Factor Pluggable) is tailored for 10G connections. CXP (C Form-Factor Pluggable, 12x10G) accommodates even higher data rates, enhancing network capacity and flexibility.</p></p>
