<p><p>Gastric Electric Stimulation (GES) devices are categorized into low frequency and high frequency types based on their stimulation range. Low frequency GES devices typically operate at frequencies below 10 Hz, aimed at enhancing gastric motility and controlling nausea in patients with gastroparesis. In contrast, high frequency GES devices operate above 10 Hz, primarily designed to reduce appetite and aid in weight management. Both types utilize electrical impulses to modify gastric function, offering therapeutic options for diverse gastrointestinal disorders.</p></p>
