<p><p>The skin implant market is categorized into two primary types based on the source of the skin used: from animal sources and from human sources. Animal-sourced skin implants, typically derived from pigs or cows, are often used for temporary wound coverage or burn treatment, providing a biological scaffold for healing. Conversely, human-sourced skin implants, harvested from donors, are used for more permanent solutions like reconstructive surgery and chronic wound care, offering better compatibility and reduced rejection rates.</p></p>
